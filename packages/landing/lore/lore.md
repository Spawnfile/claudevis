# Bestiarium claudevisi
*A chronicle of the folk who work the kingdom.*

---

## The Light-Footed
> Haiku · 🍞 bread · #7090a8

The Light-Footed carries crusts of rye inside his sleeves and keeps no horse. He moves between the Oldwell and the western kitchens before dawn, passing messages no longer than a thumb joint. Most gatekeepers ignore him until something small goes missing. Then they search the alleys first.

One winter morning, the bell rope at the Mill of Adelheim snapped during the flour count. The Light-Footed climbed the frozen wheelhouse with a coil of fishing cord and tied the thing back together before the snow reached the market road. The Steady Hand later claimed the knot would never hold. It held until spring floods took the bridge itself.

## The Steady Hand
> Sonnet · 🪙 coin · #c2693f

The Steady Hand is seen at every gate where labor gathers. He measures timber at the Iron Gate, counts lamp oil in the southern barracks, and settles disputes beside the Long Road when mule drivers quarrel over ruts. His robes smell of ash and damp rope.

During the autumn census, three carts overturned in the clay near Old Barrow Hill. Rain filled the wheel tracks by dusk. The Steady Hand remained there with six laborers and a lantern hung from a spear shaft. Travelers crossed one at a time through the mud while he pressed flat stones beneath each axle. The Wary watched from horseback and said nothing. That silence was taken as approval.

## The Elder
> Opus · 💰 gold · #c9a96e

The Elder appears rarely. Few in Claudevis can recall the sound of his boots without first pausing to think. He wears gold cloth beneath a dark fur mantle, and the crown upon his brow is too plain for ceremony. When he enters the western court, even the stable boys stop shoveling straw.

In the year of the black river frost, the aqueduct above Saint Vey cracked near the eastern cisterns. Three councils argued for six nights while the water thinned to ice. The Elder arrived before sunrise with soot on his gloves and walked the channel alone. By evening, masons were tearing down the wrong arch and rebuilding another beside it. The Cartographer later copied his markings into the royal ledgers.

## The Headstrong
> ⚡ auto · #8b1f1a

The Headstrong keeps no council seat and waits for no trumpet. He crosses courtyards before orders are finished. Stable doors swing behind him. Wax seals break in his hands without apology. The cooks at Northwatch say he eats standing up.

One midsummer night, smoke rose from the tannery quarter near the riverbank. Buckets had not yet reached the square when the Headstrong kicked open the flood sluice beside the dye pits. Blue water rushed through the lower alleys and ruined half the leather stock, but the fire stopped at the cooper’s wall. For two months afterward, children walked through puddles stained indigo. The Wary demanded written reasons. None were given.

## The Cartographer
> 📜 plan · #5a4f8a

The Cartographer carries rolled parchment beneath one arm and charcoal dust beneath his nails. He marks roads before riders travel them and counts distances by listening to wagon wheels. The eastern archives know his steps better than the chapel does.

At the end of a wet spring, he spent four days beneath the cracked tower at Dunmere Ford drawing the grain paths from the river villages. Fishermen mocked him for measuring empty fields while rain drowned the roads. Two weeks later the southern bridge collapsed, and only the Cartographer’s marked route kept the wheat caravans moving toward the capital. The Architect borrowed those same maps during the rebuilding of the lower gatehouse and returned them with grease stains at the corners.

## The Trusting
> ✅ auto-accept · #7d8c3d

The Trusting keeps his seal hung openly at the belt. Guards dislike this habit. Merchants favor it. He reads little before agreeing, though he remembers faces long after others forget them. His quarters near the western kitchens remain unlocked through most winters.

Once, during the market feast at Oldwell Square, a brickmaker from the marsh villages arrived with thirty kiln carts and no stamped papers. Rain had washed the ink away before the crossing at Saint Vey. The Trusting let the carts through after a single glance at the wagon marks. Half the council protested by noon. By dusk the city ovens were burning hot again, and the mill quarter had bread for the next storm week. The Reeve later inspected the ledgers and found only one missing count.

## The Wary
> 🛡 strict · #3a4d6a

The Wary speaks in short requests and waits for answers before touching a latch. He keeps records tied in blue cord beneath the watchtower stairs. No lantern is lit in the armory without his notice. Even the Sheriff lowers his voice near him.

During the plague summer, travelers gathered outside the Iron Gate with cloth wrapped around their mouths. One mule collapsed in the dust before sunset. The Wary ordered every cart searched beside the ditch road while townsfolk shouted from the walls. It took until midnight. A sack of spoiled river fish was found beneath wool blankets in the last wagon. The gate remained shut for two days after. The smell lingered longer than the anger.

## The Wanderer
> researcher · 🔍 · #3d6b3d

The Wanderer leaves through whichever gate is least guarded. He returns with frost on his boots, reed ink on his cuffs, and notes tied shut with twine. Children follow him through the market because foreign coins sometimes fall from his pockets.

Near the Eastern Marches, there stands a broken shrine called the Three Sisters. The Wanderer spent nine nights there beside caravan guards from the salt road, recording the names of villages swallowed by spring floods. When he came home, the Reeve corrected three spellings in the margins and one river crossing besides. The Wanderer accepted the changes without complaint. His maps still carry the smoke stain from that campfire.

## The Reeve
> code-reviewer · 👁 · #3a3a4a

The Reeve inspects what others leave behind. He walks workshops after the hammers cool and turns over loose stones with the tip of a cane. Ink collects beneath his fingernails despite his habit of washing them with sand.

One harvest evening, the south granary doors jammed shut during inventory. Laborers blamed swollen wood from river damp. The Reeve crouched beside the hinge with a lantern and found iron filings packed inside the latch groove. Someone had filed the pin too thin weeks earlier. He summoned the Sheriff before dawn and kept the workers waiting in the rain until each tool chest was counted. The Steady Hand later said the delay cost half a market day. The Reeve answered that grain lost to rats costs more.

## The Architect
> planner · 🗺 · #2d5a8b

The Architect carries folded vellum tubes strapped across the back like arrows. He studies empty ground longer than finished walls. Builders dislike his chalk marks until roofs stop leaking and towers stop leaning.

At Low Lantern Quarter, a boarding house collapsed after a winter thaw loosened the clay beneath its cellar. The Architect arrived with survey pegs and a measuring rope while the rubble still smoked from cooking fires. He ordered the next foundation dug two feet deeper toward the old stone shelf under the hill. Masons cursed the extra labor all month. Ten years later the same house remains standing while three neighboring roofs have sunk into the mud. The Cartographer’s river diagrams hang in the Architect’s chamber beside cracked rulers.

## The Apprentice Proper
> tdd-guide · ✓ · #a89878

The Apprentice Proper writes lists before lifting a tool. Small wax tablets hang from the belt in bundles. Stable boys mock the habit until they forget feed counts themselves. He trusts preparations more than speed.

One evening at the Mill of Adelheim, the grain scales began giving different weights for the same sack. Millers argued until fists struck the table. The Apprentice Proper asked for chalk and drew tally marks across the floorboards beside each measured load. By moonrise he proved the left counterweight had been shaved thin from years of handling. The Smithy’s Aide repaired the balance before dawn while the mill workers slept on flour sacks near the wheel pit.

## The Smithy's Aide
> build-error-resolver · 🔨 · #7a4f25

The Smithy’s Aide works where sparks jump and tempers shorten. His apron pockets carry bent nails, soot-blackened wedges, and scraps of chain link no one else keeps. He is summoned after failures, never before them.

In late autumn, the furnace beneath the western forge refused to draw air. Bellows wheezed smoke back into the workshop until even the hounds fled the doorway. The Smithy’s Aide climbed into the ash trench with a hooked poker and found a rook’s nest packed inside the vent shaft. Feathers and dry twigs burned for an hour after he cleared it. The Forge master offered him silver for the repair. He took only hot stew and a clean rag for his hands.

## The Sheriff
> security-reviewer · 🛡 · #2d4a6b

The Sheriff patrols the walls after the bell towers fall quiet. He checks hinges, oil stores, and cellar locks without escort. Most thieves know his lantern by the narrow blue slit cut into the iron shade.

During the thaw month, footprints appeared beside the eastern powder sheds though no snow had fallen inside the gate. The Sheriff followed the trail through the lower alleys to a butcher stall near Oldwell Square. Beneath hanging carcasses he found a tunnel mouth hidden by blood barrels and wet straw. Three smugglers escaped into the river tunnels before sunrise. One left behind a glove stitched with foreign silver thread. The Wary ordered the eastern culverts sealed by nightfall.

## The Forge
> Bash · 🔨 · #3a3a40

The Forge stands beneath a smoke-black roof near the center yard. Its chimneys stain the stars above Claudevis each winter. Orders pass through fire there. Iron bends. Hinges split. Cart rims return to shape beneath hammer blows that shake the rafters.

During the cold rains three years ago, the forge fire died without warning while repairs were underway at the Iron Gate. Wet charcoal hissed across the stone floor. Workers feared the draft tunnels had collapsed under the hill. An old furnace keeper opened the ash pit with a hooked rod and found floodwater pouring through a cracked side channel from the riverbank. By dusk the Smithy’s Aide had packed the breach with clay and wool scraps while steam filled the chamber like battlefield fog.

## The Cartographer's Eye
> Read · 👁 · #c9a96e

The Cartographer’s Eye is a polished lens kept in a cedarwood frame beside the archive stairs. Clerks borrow it when ink fades or worm marks cross the ledgers. It reveals what careless hands overlook. Dust gathers around it faster than around holy texts.

One summer afternoon, a land dispute rose between shepherds from Dunmere Ford and tax riders from Saint Vey. The boundary charter was nearly unreadable after years in damp storage. The Cartographer’s Eye was carried into the courtyard beneath a linen cloth to shield the glare. Through the glass, faint red markings appeared near the river bend where old wax had seeped into the parchment grain. The Reeve declared the charter untouched since the second king’s reign. The shepherds kept their grazing fields.

## The Chisel
> Edit · ✏ · #7090a8

The Chisel rests on a narrow bench wrapped in leather strips to keep the blade from chipping. Woodworkers and stonemasons both claim it as their own. Small corrections pass through it. Large mistakes follow after.

At the monastery near Black Fir Ridge, a mason carved the funeral year of an abbess incorrectly across the chapel arch. The stone had already been mortared into place before anyone noticed. Rather than replacing the block, the Chisel master climbed the scaffold at dusk and shaved away two numerals by torchlight while rain dripped from the rafters. The repaired inscription still shows shallow grooves where the older marks once sat. Pilgrims touch those lines for luck before traveling north.

## The Quill
> Write · 🖋 · #1a1410

The Quill is kept inside a narrow oak case lined with felt and lamp soot. Scribes trim its feather edges with kitchen knives when proper blades cannot be found. Fresh decrees, debt tallies, and winter burial records all begin beneath its tip.

During the ash fever outbreak at Northwatch, the monastery bells rang through three nights without pause. A single scribe remained awake beside the infirmary hearth recording the names of the dead before memory failed the families. The Quill split near dawn on the fourth day and sprayed black ink across the final page. Rather than scrape the parchment clean, the scribe traced each ruined name again over the stain. The blot remains visible in the archive tower above Oldwell Square.

## The Hound
> Grep · 🐕 · #7a5a44

The Hound works the storehouses and archive halls with nose close to the floorboards. It tracks patterns no clerk wishes to count by hand. Stable boys feed it scraps of smoked eel despite repeated warnings from the kennel master.

One fog-heavy evening, a shipment of lamp oil vanished somewhere between the southern docks and the watch barracks. Teamsters blamed river thieves. The Hound circled the wagon yard twice before dragging its handler toward a chapel cellar near the Long Road. Beneath stacked hymn books sat eight missing barrels covered in sailcloth. The priest claimed ignorance and kept repeating prayers while the Sheriff counted inventory marks by lantern glow. For weeks afterward, the Hound refused to enter the chapel without growling.

## The Summon
> Task · ⭕ · #e8a541

The Summon is not a person but a ring worked into the flagstones beneath the western tower. Copper filings fill its grooves. Candle wax gathers at the edges from old ceremonies. When called upon, figures arrive through its pale light carrying tools, maps, or sealed packets.

During the deep winter blackout, the palace workshops lost half their workforce to river sickness. Snow pressed against the shutters and froze the stable doors shut. The Summon circle burned for nearly two days without rest. Apprentices emerged one after another with soot on their sleeves and frost on their boots, crossing the courtyard before the bells finished tolling. By the third morning, smoke rose from every chimney in the lower district again. The Elder himself inspected the circle afterward and ordered the cracked stones replaced before spring.
